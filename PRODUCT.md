# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users
Business owners and operators in Lagos (and similar operations-heavy markets) running fragmented setups: Excel, QuickBooks, WhatsApp, paper, Trello. They land here from a warm referral, not cold search. They're evaluating whether this person can be trusted to consolidate their operations into one system, using Okoh ERP (500+ clients, Shell, CEED Supply) as the proof. Secondary audience: skincare brands evaluating Skin AI, and developers considering mentorship.

## Product Purpose
A personal/business site for Bamidele Ajibola (Alphawga), full-stack developer and technical architect. Core statement: "I consolidate scattered business operations into one intelligent system." The site converts a warm referral into a booked call, using the Okoh ERP case study as the primary proof anchor and a free operational-audit questionnaire as the lead magnet. Success = booked diagnostic calls and audit completions, not pageviews.

## Brand Personality
Confident, precise, proof-first. Leads with what's already been delivered, never with what's needed. Speaks like one person talking to one person — short, direct sentences, no question-hook headlines, no generic business jargon (leverage, streamline, synergy). Nigerian-specific context where relevant (Naira, real local tools like WhatsApp-as-ops-tool). Never invents numbers, client names, or pricing that haven't been supplied.

## Anti-references
The generic AI-coding-agent aesthetic: Inter-alone-everywhere + purple gradients, 8px-rounded drop-shadow cards, perfectly symmetrical three-column layouts, Heroicons-in-a-gradient-circle tiles, dark mode that's literally `#000000`. This site does use Inter, but paired deliberately with Fraunces (display) and IBM Plex Mono (data/labels) as a three-voice type system, not as the sole typeface — that pairing, not Inter itself, is what avoids the generic-agent tell. Also avoid generic corporate SaaS marketing template feel — this is a technical architect's site, not a startup's.

## Design Principles
- Proof before pitch: every section earns its claim with something already built (Okoh ERP), not aspirational language.
- One accent, used sparingly: brass is the entire brand voltage, not a rainbow of category colors. Steel, manila, and rust are narrow structural/reserved-purpose colors, not additional accents.
- Typography carries the hierarchy; decoration doesn't. No stock photography, no icon-and-gradient filler.
- Nigerian-specific and concrete beats generic and global. Real tools, real numbers (where supplied), real client context.
- The homepage sells the business; `/about` alone carries the fuller personal story (SchoolWave, Insight Flow, Synk framed honestly as lessons, not hidden failures).

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Standard WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast against the dark canvas, large text ≥3:1. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for the homepage's scroll-driven consolidation sequence and any audit-flow transitions. No motion-only affordances — every interactive state has a non-motion cue (color/border change) as well.
